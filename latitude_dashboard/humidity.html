Humidity

At one time or another, we've all experienced a sticky hot summer day, with air so thick you could cut it with a knife, or a bitterly cold winter where in spite of your humidifer, your lips are hopelessly chapped. Does our real life experience hold up? Do we see a correlation between latitude and humidity?

In this scatterplot, we see a relatively random distribution of humidities from about 20% to 100% irrespective of latitude. This indicates that latitude does not have a significant impact on a region's humidity. There is an interesting collection of points clustered at 0% humidity at ~70 degrees latitude, lower than any other points by ~10%. The 70th parallel is in the Arctic, and humidity tends to be very low when temperatures are cold and sea ice covers the Arctic Ocean. Unfortunately, we don't have equivalent data points at -70 degrees latitude to compare it to, as this parallel falls in Antarctica.
