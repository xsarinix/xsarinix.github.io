Wind Speed

The two main causes of large-scale atmospheric circulation is the difference in temperature between the equator and the poles and the Coriolis effect from the rotation of the planet. Locally, these air currents interact with terrain and the water cycle to form breezes, gusts, gales, squalls... in other words, wind! Knowing the role that temperature plays, do we see a correlation between latitude and wind speed?

In this scatterplot, we see a relatively random distribution of points from 1-10mph that thins out between 10-16, irrespective of latitude. This indicates that latitude does not have a significant impact on a region's wind speed. However, this graph still yields usefull information thanks to the density of points between 2-8mph with speeds stretching up to 17mph, from which we can deduce that on this day, the average wind speed across the world on this day was likely somewhere between 6-10 mph.
